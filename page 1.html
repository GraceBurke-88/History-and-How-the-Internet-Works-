Internet Privacy

How Privacy Risks Impact Young-Adults

Now more than ever, a large amount of people, namley young-adults, spend a good portion of their day on the Internet. Would you be comfortable with knowing everything you search for, look at, and send? Personally, I would have to say no. However, did you know that the government actually collects all of this data? Privacy, nowadays, is a relative thing, and as so, we must learn safe internet browsing methods to protect ourselves online, and avoid getting in trouble. It is important to know that you do have a certain level of control over your own privacy, if only you devote time to practice some habits covered in later pages.

Our Privacy is deeply at Risk

It is clear that our privacy is deeply at risk in the ways that we utilize the internet today. From using social media, to shopping online, all of the browsers we use are tracking us and using that information to make profits off of our metadata. As consumers, we should be more aware of where our data is going and what information is being taken from us. It may not be possible to ditch social media for good, or totally go off the grid, but we still have some power to take our digital security into our own hands.

Be Informed!

Informing yourself of issues like this is the first step of learning to protect yourself online, and keep what you want to keep, private. So keep reading, and stay safe!
